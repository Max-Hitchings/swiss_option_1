Daily Itinerary

Day 1: Zurich City Adventure & Limmat River Float

Morning: Sweet Start

Begin your Swiss journey at the Lindt Home of Chocolate. Arrive at 10:00 AM to explore interactive exhibits and indulge in chocolate sampling. Afterwards, grab a quick brunch in Zurich's charming old town.

Afternoon: Float the Limmat

Experience Zurich like a local by floating down the Limmat River. Opt for a DIY float from Flussbad Oberer Letten or join a guided raft tour for a more relaxed 2-3 hour journey through the city.

Evening: Old Town Charm

Explore Zurich's Altstadt (Old Town) on foot. Enjoy dinner at a traditional restaurant, perhaps trying Zürcher Geschnetzeltes, and end the night with a panoramic city view from Lindenhof.

Day 2: Rhine Falls & Into the Bernese Oberland

Morning: Europe's Largest Waterfall

Drive 40 mins to Rhine Falls. Feel the power from the viewing platforms, then take the yellow boat to the central rock for a thrilling 360° viewpoint surrounded by thundering water.

Afternoon: Valley of 72 Waterfalls

Drive ~2 hours to the Interlaken area. Explore the stunning Lauterbrunnen Valley, visiting the incredible Trümmelbach Falls (glacial waterfalls inside a mountain) and the iconic Staubbach Falls.

Evening: Trek Preparation

Enjoy a hearty dinner in Lauterbrunnen (fondue is a must!). Repack your gear into a smaller backpack for the 3-day trek. Park your car securely in Lauterbrunnen or Stechelberg.

Day 3: Hut-to-Hut Trek Day 1 – Mürren to Griesalp

The Trek Begins

A challenging 16km (~6.5 hours) hike from the car-free village of Mürren. The goal is to cross the Sefinenfurgge Pass (2,612m). The trail involves a +1050m ascent and -1200m descent, with some steep sections assisted by stairs and cables.

Evening: Griesalp Arrival

Descend into the remote Kiental valley and arrive at Griesalp. Check into your pre-booked mountain inn (e.g., Berggasthaus Golderli). Enjoy a well-deserved, hearty alpine dinner and rest up for the next stage.

Day 4: Hut-to-Hut Trek Day 2 – Griesalp to Blümlisalphütte

Morning: The Royal Stage

Today is the toughest but most rewarding day. A punishing ascent of +1,370m over just 5km to cross the Hohtürli Pass (2,778m). The final push involves long wooden staircases bolted into the rock.

Afternoon/Evening: Sleeping Among Giants

Arrive at the Blümlisalphütte (2,843m). Enjoy lunch with jaw-dropping views of glaciers and peaks. Spend the night in this traditional Swiss Alpine Club hut for an unforgettable high-altitude experience. Remember your sleeping bag liner!

Day 5: Trek Day 3 – Oeschinensee & Car Retrieval

Morning: Descent to Paradise

Wake up to a sunrise alpenglow. Hike down from the hut to the stunning, turquoise Oeschinensee Lake. Reward your tired legs with a refreshing (and cold!) swim or rent a rowboat. Or... we leave Oeschinensee Lake early and go to Grindelwald First to do zip line, mountain cart and cliff walk

Afternoon: Car Retrieval & Return

Take the gondola down to Kandersteg. Time for logistics: take a train back to Lauterbrunnen to retrieve the car. Once reunited, drive back to your base in the Interlaken area. Consider a quick stop at the magical Blausee (Blue Lake).

Day 6: Interlaken Adventure Trio

Morning: Canyoning

Get your adrenaline pumping with a canyoning trip in Grimsel canyon. Rappel 50 meters, jump off ledges, slide down natural rock chutes, and zipline over chasms. A true adventure!

Afternoon: Paragliding

Take to the skies for a tandem paragliding flight over Interlaken. Soar like a bird with incredible views of Lake Thun, Lake Brienz, and the high Alps. Landing is in the center of town.

Evening: Sunset Paddleboarding

Wind down with a tranquil stand-up paddleboard (SUP) session on Lake Brienz or Lake Thun at sunset. Consider a guided tour with LED-lit boards and Swiss wine tasting for a magical end to the day.

Day 7: Scenic Drive & Departure

Morning: Scenic Drive to Zurich

Check out and begin the 2-hour drive back to Zurich. If time permits, take a detour to explore Bern's UNESCO-listed Old Town, the Emmentaler Show Dairy, or the beautiful city of Lucerne via the scenic Brünig Pass.

Afternoon: Departure

Arrive in the Zurich area. Do some last-minute souvenir shopping on Bahnhofstrasse before heading to the airport (ZRH) to return your rental car and catch your flight. Uf Wiederluege, Switzerland!

Trek & Driving Logistics

Parking During Trek: We recommend Lauterbrunnen’s garage (~CHF 18/day +). Cheaper options are available at Stechelberg (~CHF 12/day) or Wilderswil (~CHF 5/day), but require an extra train leg.

Driving Tips: Your rental car will have a highway vignette (toll sticker). Stick to speed limits (cameras are common). Use lower gears on mountain descents to save brakes. Yield to postbuses on tight roads.

Parking Tips: Use blue zones for short stays (with a parking disc) or covered garages for longer periods. Carry coins for meters and consider downloading the EasyPark app.

Packing List

Clothing

  • Broken-in hiking boots & wool socks
  • Quick-dry hiking clothes & warm layers (fleece)
  • Waterproof rain jacket
  • Sleeping bag liner (required for huts)
  • Warm hat & light gloves
  • Casual city clothes & swimwear
  • Sandals/slip-ons for after hiking

Gear & Essentials

  • 30-40L backpack for the trek
  • Trekking poles (highly recommended)
  • 1L water bottle & thermos
  • Headlamp & extra batteries
  • Sunglasses, sun hat, and high-SPF sunscreen
  • Type J travel adapter & power bank
  • Compact first-aid kit with blister pads
  • Trail snacks & electrolytes
  • Cash (CHF) for small purchases
  • Dry bag or ziplock pouches
